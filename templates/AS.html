Alexandra Sablina

Background

Musical Journey

Alexandra began her musical journey in 4th grade, switching from clarinet to saxophone and immediately falling in love with the instrument's expressive voice. She was drawn to the saxophone's ability to convey emotion and tell stories without words. What started as a school band class quickly became a meaningful part of her life. Over time, she expanded her skills to include alto, tenor, and baritone saxophones. Her musical experiences have helped her grow not only as a performer but also as a leader and mentor in the music community. Alexandra views music as a lifelong pursuit—one that brings people together and sparks creativity, confidence, and joy.

Performance Experience

Alexandra is a dedicated and experienced performer who has participated in a wide range of musical ensembles. She currently plays in the Woodinville High School Wind Ensemble and Jazz Band, where she also serves as the secretary and tenor saxophone section leader. In her role as music curator for the jazz band, she helps select and explore pieces that introduce new styles and broaden her peers’ musical understanding. Alexandra has also played in the pit orchestra for theatrical productions like Footloose, where she learned the nuances of timing, precision, and collaboration in a live setting. In addition to large ensembles, she enjoys playing in chamber groups, where intimate teamwork and musicianship are key. Her well-rounded background has prepared her to approach all kinds of performances with versatility and professionalism.

Teaching Philosophy

Alexandra is a thoughtful and supportive teacher who believes in meeting students where they are and helping them grow at their own pace. She combines a structured approach with creativity, working to build strong technical fundamentals while also nurturing musical curiosity. Her teaching emphasizes the importance of problem-solving, encouraging students to understand their instrument deeply so they can grow more independently. Alexandra aims to make each lesson feel rewarding and inspiring, adapting her instruction based on each student’s learning style, goals, and interests. Whether the student is a beginner learning to make their first sounds or an advanced player preparing for an audition, Alexandra brings patience, insight, and enthusiasm to every lesson. Her ultimate goal is to help students develop not just as musicians, but as confident individuals who find joy and purpose through music.
