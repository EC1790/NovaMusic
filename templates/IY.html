Isaac Yu

Background

Musical Journey

Isaac’s musical path began in 2nd grade with the guitar, where he first developed his appreciation for rhythm and sound. In 4th grade, he transitioned to the drums, which introduced him to the joy of dynamic expression and ensemble playing. Eventually, in 9th grade, Isaac found his primary passion in the horn, where he discovered the full depth of musical tone, control, and melodic storytelling. Today, Isaac plays both drums and horn with skill and enthusiasm, enjoying the versatility of mastering both rhythm and melody. His journey through different instruments has given him a unique, well-rounded musical perspective that informs both his playing and his teaching.

Performance Experience

Isaac has built a rich performance portfolio over the years, starting with solo and ensemble competitions in both middle and high school. His talent has led him to perform in some of the most prestigious venues in Washington, including Benaroya Hall and Meany Hall in Seattle—settings that require strong musical discipline and stage presence. In addition to local performances, Isaac is taking his music international, touring Portugal in the summer to perform across the country. This wide range of experience has taught him how to connect with audiences, adapt to different performance settings, and embrace music as a global language.

Teaching Philosophy

Isaac brings a calm, focused, and innovative approach to teaching. He believes that students learn best when lessons are both structured and flexible—anchored in strong fundamentals, yet always evolving with fresh content and engaging material. Isaac is attentive to each student’s learning style and pace, ensuring that they stay motivated and continue to grow as musicians. His approach balances consistency with creativity, making sure that students are always developing their technique while staying excited about their musical journey. Isaac’s goal is to inspire students to find joy and pride in their playing while equipping them with the skills to continue learning for years to come.
