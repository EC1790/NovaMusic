Jillian Harada

Background

Musical Journey

Jillian began playing piano at age five, starting with informal lessons from her grandmother, a highly experienced piano teacher and competition judge. Over the past 12 years, she has received formal training, developing both technical skill and musicality. After completing all levels of the ABRSM exams with distinction, she further improved her sightreading and musical expression through film music arrangements. She is currently focused on refining technique with advanced repertoire such as Chopin’s etudes.

Performance Experience

Jillian has performed in annual recitals since early elementary school and has passed all eight levels of the ABRSM exams with distinction. These exams included performance, scales, sightreading, listening tests, and music theory, demonstrating a well-rounded musical education. While she has stepped back from competitions, she previously earned a bronze medal in the National League of Performing Arts international piano competition. She also has experience accompanying other musicians, including violinists, cellists, and pianists.

Teaching Philosophy

Jillian approaches piano teaching from both technical and musical perspectives—focusing on finger precision as well as expression and interpretation. She draws on her own learning experience to support beginners and has given feedback to students at a variety of skill levels. With a strong background in teaching other subjects, she brings patience and clarity to her instruction. She also continues to refine her teaching methods through mentorship from her grandmother.
